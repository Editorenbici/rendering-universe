# Energy and Pressure Definitions for Scalar Fields in Disformal Theories

**Date:** 2026-07-03

**Sources (exact arXiv only, no speculation):**

- J. Sakstein, "Disformal Theories of Gravity: From the Solar System to Cosmology", arXiv:1409.1734
- M. Zumalacárregui et al., "Disformal Scalar Fields and the Dark Sector of the Universe", arXiv:1004.2684
- D. Bettoni and S. Liberati, "Disformal invariance of second order scalar-tensor theories: Framing the Horndeski action", arXiv:1306.6724
- Related k-essence and galileon disformal couplings referenced in above and standard literature (e.g., DBI Galileons in Einstein frame contexts linked in searches).

## Standard Definitions of ρ and p

In the Einstein frame for the scalar field with Lagrangian L = X - V(φ), where X = - (1/2) ∇_μ φ ∇^μ φ (Sakstein arXiv:1409.1734):

T_φ μν = (1/(8πG)) [ ∇_μ φ ∇_ν φ - δ_μ^ν ( (1/2) ∇_μ φ ∇^μ φ + V(φ) ) ]

For homogeneous FLRW background, this corresponds to perfect fluid with:

ρ_φ = (1/2) \dot φ² + V(φ)

p_φ = (1/2) \dot φ² - V(φ)

 (Note: in units where the action coefficient leads to this; general form for k-essence L = P(φ, X) is ρ = 2 X P_,X - P , p = P (Zumalacárregui arXiv:1004.2684))

In disformal coupling, matter energy-momentum tensor T_m ^μν is defined w.r.t. the Jordan (disformal) metric \tilde g_μν.

Relation between frames (Sakstein arXiv:1409.1734):

T_m ^μν = A^6 (φ) √(1 - 2 B² X / Λ²) \tilde T_m ^μν

The effective density and pressure of matter in Einstein frame are rescaled versions; the conserved density is the Jordan frame one.

In the disformal scalar field model (Zumalacárregui arXiv:1004.2684), after transformation, the effective Lagrangian leads to:

ρ = (1 / √ℒ) ( ε \dot φ² / (2 ℒ) + V )

p = √ℒ ( ε \dot φ² / (2 ℒ) - V )

where ℒ = 1 - B \dot φ² is the lapse function measuring relative time flow in disformal vs Einstein frame.

This reduces to standard k-essence forms when B=0.

For galileon and Horndeski disformal (Bettoni arXiv:1306.6724 and linked papers), the effective fluid description for the scalar includes higher-order terms, but the background ρ and p are extracted from the Friedmann equations as the contributions to the total energy density and pressure from the galileon/Horndeski Lagrangian terms (standard effective fluid for DE in these theories).

## Relation like E=mc² for the field

No exact "E=mc²" relation for the scalar field itself is reported in the sources as a general feature (scalar fields are relativistic fields, not particles with rest mass in that sense).

For test particles/matter coupled to the disformal metric (Bekenstein transformation in arXiv:gr-qc/9211017 and Sakstein), the particle action is S = - m ∫ dτ where dτ is the proper time w.r.t. the physical (Jordan/disformal) metric.

This leads to effective rest energy modified by the conformal factor A(φ), similar to conformal scalar-tensor theories: m_eff ≈ A(φ) m , so rest energy E_rest ≈ A(φ) m c² (with additional derivative terms from disformal part affecting moving particles).

In the weak field/static limit, it reduces to standard Newtonian with possible fifth forces, but the base is the modified proper time.

In the disformal case, for non-relativistic particles, the effective energy is influenced by the lapse-like term in the metric.

## Summary from sources only

- In Einstein frame, scalar contributions use standard k-essence/ quintessence ρ, p as above.
- Matter ρ, p defined w.r.t. disformal metric; frame transformation given explicitly in Sakstein.
- Effective descriptions in k-essence limits and galileon disformal couplings follow the 2X P_X - P form or generalized from the Lagrangian variation.
- No universal E=mc² reported for the scalar field; particle rest energies modified via the metric factors in the action.
