## Model Format for Paper A References (short technical CST numerical preprints)

Looked for style similar to Glaser 2023 "Computer simulations of causal sets" (arXiv:2306.09904) — concise, focused on numerical methods/results for CST observables (abundances, action, manifoldlikeness), ~10-20 pages, heavy on figures/tables of simulations, clear pre-reg or protocol sections.

Recommended 2-3 models (verified arXiv):

1. **Glaser, L. (2023)** "Computer simulations of causal sets", arXiv:2306.09904.  
   Structure: Intro to observables, exact analytic for flat, simulation protocol, results for manifold vs non-manifold, figures of abundances/action, conclusions on tests. Short, technical, self-contained. Excellent model for abundance work.

2. **Adamson & Wallden (2026)** "Benincasa-Dowker-Glaser causal set actions by quantum counting", arXiv:2505.22217.  
   Short technical: describes BDG action, proposes quantum counting method, complexity analysis, numerical validation, figures. Good for precise numerical claims + provenance of method.

3. **Moradi et al. (2025)** "Fluctuations and correlations in causal set theory", arXiv:2407.03395.  
   Focuses on exact computation of variances/correlations for interval abundances and action. Protocol for decomposition, results on ensembles, appendices with derivations. Concise numerical + analytic hybrid style.

These are good templates for length (~10-15 effective pages), structure (method → controls → main measurement → honest limitations), and citation density for technical CST numerical papers. Avoid long reviews; keep Paper A technical sections in this spirit (methods + tables/figures + provenance).
